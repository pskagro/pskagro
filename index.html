About Our Farm

Integrated **Domestic** Animal Unit In One Campus.

The farm combines dairy, small ruminants and backyard poultry on a rotational grazing and stall feeding system to utilize land and fodder efficiently. [web:7][web:10]

Manure from sheds is processed into organic compost and slurry for crop fields, thereby reducing external fertilizer requirement and improving soil health. [web:11]

Veterinary Monitoring

Periodic health records are maintained for each animal including vaccination, calving and production data.

Balanced Feeding

Rations are formulated with green fodder, dry fodder and concentrates to meet energy and protein needs of every age group.

Farm Workflow

Daily **Management** Cycle On The Farm.

Work is scheduled in fixed time blocks so that feeding, milking, cleaning and health care activities are not missed.

1
Morning feeding & cleaning

Fresh fodder, concentrates and clean water are supplied, followed by removal of dung and wet bedding from all sheds.

2
Milking & egg collection

Hygienic milking is performed in fixed slots; eggs are collected and graded to maintain product traceability.

3
Health checks & records

Animals are observed for appetite, movement and body condition; any abnormality is noted and attended by veterinarians.

4
Evening schedule

Evening feed, second milking and final checks are completed before animals are settled for the night.
